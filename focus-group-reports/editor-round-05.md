# Editor Round 5 — March 29, 2026

## KEY FINDING: "Correct" ≠ "True"

After 4 rounds of fixes, MIWO produces **technically correct journalism** that won't embarrass you. But it can't yet produce journalism that moves readers because it hasn't moved beyond rule-compliance into investigation.

## REMAINING ISSUES (post all fixes)

1. **Lede sourcing opaque** — vivid scenes without attribution ("Raya the radiologist" — who told us?)
2. **Secondary sources become primary facts** — Al Jazeera's death tracker is journalism about journalism, not a primary count
3. **Observed facts mixed with forecasts** — "6,000 tonnes at port" (observed) next to "18% decline projected" (forecast) violates confidence separation
4. **Causal claims without mechanism** — "evacuation orders caused displacement" but HOW were orders communicated?
5. **Economic impact numbers are estimates treated as facts** — "2-3 million kilos per week" is an exporter estimate
6. **"Want more?" section unsourced** — dangled stories with zero links

## VERDICT: 6.2/10 — Structurally sound. Editorially honest. Not yet powerful.

## PROMPT IMPLICATIONS
The system needs guidance on separating OBSERVED from ESTIMATED from PROJECTED within the same story. Current rules handle this at the sentence level but not at the paragraph level where mixing still occurs.
